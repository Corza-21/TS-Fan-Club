"Speak Now" is Taylor Swift's 3rd album, released on October 25th, 2010. After being hit with accusations from the media that Taylor wasn't pulling her weight in those writing sessions, she decided that she would write her third studio album all by herself, which she did!!
This album features 14 tracks from the standard version of the album, with 3 additional bonus tracks, including "If This Was A Movie" which Taylor wrote with Liz Rose (Which is debatably a "Fearless (Taylor's Version)" song anyway), and 2 extra tracks which are acoustic versions of "Back To December" and "Haunted".

This album starts to see Taylor leaning towards the pop space more than before. It is still considered a country-pop album, but some see it as an entirely pop album.

This album features some of her most vulnerable songs in her entire discography, and the has the longest bridge in her discography - belonging to "Long Live". This album was given 6 music videos and even a fragrance named after a lyric from "Enchanted" - "Wonderstruck" which is discontinued now.
