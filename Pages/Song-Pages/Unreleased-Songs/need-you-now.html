About

From the Taylor Swift Wiki

This song is ✨Unreleased✨.

“Need You Now” is one of the many leaked unreleased songs by Taylor Swift from her early years as a a young singer-songwriter in Nashville. Written by Swift in her teens, the song was co-written with one of her early collaborators, Robert Ellis Orrall, who also co-wrote with Swift and Angelo Petraglia on the album track from her eponymous debut album, “A Place In This World”.

The song is about moving on from someone who couldn't reciprocate the love, patience and respect you have invested in the relationship despite this person trying to win you back again by connecting with your common friends and personally paying you a visit to your house.

“Need You Now” is another one of those ironic Taylor Swift song titles such as “Forever & Always”, from the 2010 GRAMMY Album of the Year awardee, “Fearless”, as the song depicts someone confronting an ex-partner and declaring that they actually no longer need them and their broken promises and neglect of the relationship.

Fans have noticed the song's similarities to Swift's acclaimed album track, “All Too Well”, from her fourth studio album, “Red”.
