About

Track 25 on "Red".

"Message In A Bottle (Taylor's Version) [From The Vault]" is Swift's admission of fascination and admiration for a new potential lover. Although frightening, this new infatuation carries with it hopes of being true love. The song seems to be connected thematically to "Come Backâ€¦ Be Here," which talks about Swift struggling to have a long-distance relationship with someone. It is rumored that the subject of both songs is Harry Styles, whom Swift dated towards the end of 2012.

A "message in a bottle" is a form of communication used by castaways looking for rescue. Similarly, Taylor uses it to express her readiness for commitment to someone seemingly far away for now, specifically in "London," conveniently being the capital city of Harry Styles' home country. With the outro's lyrics differing from those in the chorus, it seems that this song is an actual "message in a bottle" to her potential lover.

The song's pop sound, with an upbeat tempo brought on by drums and a guitar, is very familiar to songs on Swift's 1989. The similarities between this song and ones on 1989 make sense, since this is the first track Taylor wrote with Max Martin and Shellback. Both producers contributed on many songs on 1989, most notably "Wildest Dreams," "Style," "Blank Space" and "Shake It Off."
