Track 12 on "Midnights".

"Sweet Nothing," the twelfth track on Midnights, is a slow, soft love song which seems to reflect the concept of soft words exchanged between lovers that are often called "sweet nothings". The song follows Taylor's simple gratitude and love of the life that she has built with boyfriend Joe Alwyn. Swift speaks of how she finds peace in her romantic relationship with her partner who asks "sweet nothing" of her as opposed to the pressures she faces from the media, a topic Swift has written about frequently, namely in her 2017 album reputation and the Midnights lead single "Anti-Hero".

The song is written by Taylor Swift and Joe Alwyn under the alias, William Bowery. This is not the first time that Alwyn/Bowery has been featured as a writer on one of Swift's tracks. Bowery has writing credits on folklore's track 4, "exile", and track 14 "betty", and evermore's "champagne problems", "coney island", and the titular closing track "evermore".
