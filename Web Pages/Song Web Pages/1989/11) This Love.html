Track 11 on "1989".

The 11th track on Taylor Swift's 1989 finds her using cyclical metaphors to ponder on the cycles of her relationship. Sometimes it's good, sometimes it's bad, but regardless, it's still love.

This was the first song Swift wrote for 1989, right after she released, "Red". The song started off as a short poem that Taylor wrote in her book:

This love is good, this love is bad
This love is alive back from the dead
These hands had to let it go free
And this love came back to me

After she wrote it in her book, Taylor started to think of it as a melody, and realised that the poem could become a song.

Also, This Love is the only songnto be solely written by her on 1989. However, she did co-write every song on the album, with "Style" having the most writers: four.

In an interview, Swift told how she decided to change who she would fall in love with:

[I'm] not looking for anything, not necessarily being open to anything, but only being open to the idea that, if I found someone who would never try to change me, that would be the only person I could fall in love with.
