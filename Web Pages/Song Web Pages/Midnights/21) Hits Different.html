Track 21 on "Midnights".

"Hits Different" is a bonus track on the Target-exclusive Midnights: Lavender Edition.

This song appears to be about a person who is struggling with the aftermath of a break-up or the end of a relationship. The speaker reflects on the pain and heartache they are experiencing, and how the end of the relationship feels different and more difficult to cope with than past break-ups. They mention feeling physically sick and unable to move on, and express sadness and longing for the person they loved.

The song also touches on the theme of memory and nostalgia, with the speaker recalling specific details and moments from the relationship. The chorus repeats the line "it hits different" to emphasize the unique and intense emotions the speaker is feeling. Overall, the song conveys the raw and difficult emotions that can come with the end of a relationship.

While Taylor has previously shunned lovers who left her such as in "White Horse" and "Picture to Burn", here, Taylor describes a situation of struggling to move on and wanting an ex back, similar to "Death by a Thousand Cuts".
